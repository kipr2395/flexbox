FactZzz About Sleep

Humans spend 1/3 of their life sleeping, which is about 25 years.

20 minutes is the perfect amount of time to nap, but 30 minutes is long enough to put you in a deep sleep that will leave you feeling groggy when you wake up.

It's impossible to sneeze while sleeping.

Within 5 minutes of waking up, 50% of your dream is forgotten. Within 10 minutes, you forget 90%.

The sensation of falling when half asleep and jerking yourself awake is called ‘hypnic jerks’.

People who play video games are more likely to be lucid dreamers, having control over their actions in dreams, than those who don't.

12% of people dream entirely in black and white.

We only dream about faces we have already seen.

The average person falls asleep in seven minutes.

More than 1,500 deaths a year are caused by drivers falling asleep at the wheel.

Sleep deprivation will kill you more quickly than food deprivation.

Depression can cause you to dream up to 3 to 4 times more than you normally would.

The higher the altitude, the greater the sleep disruption. Generally, sleep disturbance becomes greater at altitudes of 13,200 feet or more.

A new baby typically results in 400–750 hours of lost sleep for parents in the first year.

Women experience significantly more nightmares than men.

Violent dreams may be an early sign of brain disorders down the line, including Parkinson's disease and dementia.

Migrating birds can sleep while flying.

Sea otters hold hands when they sleep so they don't drift away from each other.

Only 1/2 of a dolphin's brain goes to sleep at a time.

A giraffe only needs 1.9 hours of sleep a day in 5-10 minute sessions.

Cats sleep for 70% of their lives.

Horses can sleep standing.

Rabbits often sleep with their eyes open.
